# Resources

<div class="description" style="text-align: justify;">
    <p>This page gathers references and resources related to Tashlhiyt and Amazigh languages, with a particular focus on language technology and computational linguistics. The collection includes AI models, speech and text corpora, academic venues, journals, books, articles, tools, and community initiatives. The goal is to centralize useful material for researchers, developers, students, and contributors interested in Amazigh languages, especially for NLP related tasks.</p>
    <p>The list is not exhaustive and is intended to evolve over time as new resources become available. Contributions and suggestions are welcome.</p>
</div>

<!-- Body -->

## Corpora
---
<ul>
    <li>
        <strong>Lingua Libre (Tashlhiyt)</strong>: pronunciation recordings in Tashlhiyt collected via the Lingua Libre project and published on Wikimedia Commons
        [<a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Lingua_Libre_pronunciation-shi">Website</a>].
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Tatoeba (Tashlhit)</strong>: sentence-level text data in Tashlhit, suitable for linguistic analysis and NLP research
        [<a href="https://tatoeba.org/en/downloads">Tatoeba Downloads</a>].
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Bible-based TTS Datasets</strong>: speech recordings in Standard Tamazight with two speakers
        (speaker&nbsp;1
        [<a href="https://huggingface.co/datasets/HackAI-2025/tts_dataset_speaker1">HuggingFace</a>],
        speaker&nbsp;2
        [<a href="https://huggingface.co/datasets/HackAI-2025/tts_dataset_speaker2">HuggingFace</a>]).
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Tamazight Speech Datasets (Collection)</strong>: a curated collection of Tamazight speech corpora for speech processing tasks
        [<a href="https://huggingface.co/collections/Tamazight-NLP/speech-datasets-6564886cb6c4673589355136">HuggingFace Collection</a>].
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Tashlhiyt–Darija Corpus</strong>: a parallel speech corpus for Tashlhiyt and Moroccan Darija, with Tashlhiyt transcribed in Arabic script
        [<a href="https://huggingface.co/datasets/NoureddineMOR/Tashlhiyt-darija">HuggingFace</a>,
        <a href="https://aclanthology.org/2025.icnlsp-1.37.pdf">Paper</a>].
    </li>
</ul>


## AI Models
---
<ul>
    <li>
        <strong>Massively Multilingual Speech (MMS)</strong> by Meta supports Tashlhiyt (see
        <a href="https://dl.fbaipublicfiles.com/mms/misc/language_coverage_mms.html">Languages</a>)
        for both ASR and TTS
        [<a href="https://huggingface.co/facebook/mms-1b-all">HuggingFace</a>].
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Amazigh-TTS</strong> is a Latin-script adaptation of the MMS model
        [<a href="https://huggingface.co/spaces/ayymen/Amazigh-tts">HuggingFace</a>].
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Tamazight TTS</strong> is a Coqui TTS model trained on a Bible corpus in Standard Tamazight
        [<a href="https://huggingface.co/spaces/Tamazight-NLP/TTS">HuggingFace</a>].
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Omnilingual ASR (Fairseq2)</strong> is a massively multilingual ASR system developed by Meta, with training data covering over one thousand languages, including Tashlhiyt
(see Table&nbsp;25 in the
<a href="https://scontent-cdg4-1.xx.fbcdn.net/v/t39.2365-6/580017083_853013560593475_1790668029330002512_n.pdf?_nc_cat=104&ccb=1-7&_nc_sid=3c67a6&_nc_ohc=khVt0OtmCQ4Q7kNvwFSXGmu&_nc_oc=AdmM89aw8JQV1pqu7hrmVuhf8CoYnrcjxnkwMo4YfY7GKgRrEqPNNPa0Vbe9pvizY8E&_nc_zt=14&_nc_ht=scontent-cdg4-1.xx&_nc_gid=E09RZCJgLFhtYKmNCZ2j4w&oh=00_AfkI9_Htu5NRmQLmzJO4X1oeJ246y1ZoMerNMuOFFWfPEA&oe=695B2368">paper</a>)
[<a href="https://github.com/facebookresearch/omnilingual-asr">GitHub</a>,
 <a href="https://ai.meta.com/blog/omnilingual-asr-advancing-automatic-speech-recognition/">Website</a>].

    </li>
</ul>

## Researchers
---
<ul style="text-align: justify;">
    <li>
        <strong>Karim Bensoukas</strong>, a linguist whose work focuses on Berber morphology and syntax, with particular contributions to the grammatical description of Tashlhiyt and related Amazigh varieties.
        [<a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=LXoxcHQAAAAJ">Google Scholar</a>]
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Ahmed Boukous</strong>, a leading Amazigh linguist and sociolinguist, known for his work on Amazigh language standardization, language policy, and descriptive studies of Moroccan Amazigh varieties, including Tashlhiyt.
        [<a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=fr&user=4VzIddUAAAAJ&view_op=list_works&sortby=pubdate">Google Scholar</a>]
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Edmond Destaing</strong>, an early 20th-century scholar who produced foundational descriptive and lexicographic work on Berber languages, including significant studies on Shilha (Tashlhiyt).
        <!-- [<a href="">Google Scholar</a>] -->
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>François Dell</strong>, a phonologist best known for his influential work on Tashlhiyt phonology, especially syllable structure, prosody, and the interaction between phonology and phonetics.
        <!-- [<a href="">Google Scholar</a>] -->
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Mohamed Elmedlaoui</strong>, a specialist in Berber phonology and morphology, co-author of seminal work on Tashlhiyt syllable structure and a major contributor to theoretical analyses of Amazigh sound systems.
        <!-- [<a href="">Google Scholar</a>] -->
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Abdallah El Mountassir</strong>, a linguist whose research addresses Amazigh linguistics, language teaching, and sociolinguistic aspects of Tashlhiyt and other Moroccan Amazigh varieties.
        <!-- [<a href="">Google Scholar</a>] -->
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Mohamed Lahrouchi</strong>, a theoretical linguist working on phonology and morphology, including analyses of Amazigh languages, with research touching on Tashlhiyt within broader Berber and Afroasiatic frameworks.
        [<a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=C8-IzsUAAAAJ&view_op=list_works&sortby=pubdate">Google Scholar</a>]
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Rachid Ridouane</strong>, a phonetician and phonologist known for detailed acoustic and articulatory studies of Tashlhiyt, particularly on consonant gemination, timing, and phonetic typology.
        [<a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=x1SJrTIAAAAJ&view_op=list_works&sortby=pubdate">Google Scholar</a>]
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Harry Stroomer</strong>, a linguist and editor who worked extensively on Berber texts and corpora, including the publication and analysis of Tashlhiyt materials from historical archives.
        [<a href="https://www.universiteitleiden.nl/en/staffmembers/harry-stroomer#tab-1">Institutional Profile</a>]
    </li>
</ul>
